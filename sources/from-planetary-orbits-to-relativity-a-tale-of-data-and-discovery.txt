His data, which was much more accurate than anything that had been collected before, was essential in the development of Kepler’s laws of planetary motion. These laws describe the orbits of the planets around the sun and were based on Brahe’s observations of the positions of the planets over time.\n![](https://miro.medium.com/v2/resize:fit:700/1*bzULXgKNDhspGB13d7rUPw.jpeg)\nKepler (src: [https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Johannes_Kepler#/media/File:JKepler.jpg](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Johannes_Kepler#/media/File:JKepler.jpg))\nBefore Brahe, astronomers had struggled to accurately measure the positions of the planets due to the limitations of their instruments. Brahe’s observations, which were much more precise than those of his predecessors, allowed **Kepler** to develop his laws, which described the shapes of the planetary orbits and the relationship between their distance from the sun and their periods.\n![](https://miro.medium.com/v2/resize:fit:700/1*HC6hxXOSUYD0-1WxL32OjA.gif)\nKepler’s laws (src:[https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kepler%27s_laws_of_planetary_motion#/media/File:Ellipitical_orbit_of_planet_with_an_eccentricty_of_0.5.gif](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kepler%27s_laws_of_planetary_motion#/media/File:Ellipitical_orbit_of_planet_with_an_eccentricty_of_0.5.gif))\nThese laws, which were published in the early 17th century, were a major milestone in the development of classical mechanics and paved the way for the work of Newton.\n![](https://miro.medium.com/v2/resize:fit:700/1*qerhSDRDFZhAZnbDOAhcPg.jpeg)\nNewtown (src: [https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Isaac_Newton#/media/File:Portrait_of_Sir_Isaac_Newton,_1689.jpg](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Isaac_Newton#/media/File:Portrait_of_Sir_Isaac_Newton,_1689.jpg))\n**Newton**’s laws of motion, which were published in the late 17th century, were based on a series of experiments and observations that he conducted, including his famous experiments with falling bodies. These laws describe the fundamental principles of motion and form the foundation of classical physics. They are used to explain a wide range of phenomena, from the motion of the planets to the behavior of objects on Earth. Like the laws of planetary motion developed by Kepler, Newton’s laws were based on a careful analysis of data collected through experiments and observations.\nThough, according to legend, Newton was sitting under an apple tree when an apple fell and hit him on the head. It is not clear whether the incident with the apple actually happened, and even if it did, it is unlikely that it was the sole inspiration for Newton’s work on gravity.\n![](https://miro.medium.com/v2/resize:fit:700/1*lnOklGCHo7GOJ48d_5u8nw.jpeg)\nEinstein (src: [https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Albert_Einstein](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Albert_Einstein))\n**Einstein**’s theory of relativity, which was published in the early 20th century, revolutionized our understanding of space and time.
